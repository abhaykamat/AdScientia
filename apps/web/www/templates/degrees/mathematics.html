Mathematics

Four Year Undergraduate Degree Programme - MSc/MMath

Mathematics, as a subject, concerns itself with the abstract concepts of Quantity, Structure, Shape and Change [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics]. The AdScientia Mathematics degree course provides resources equivalent to a top-tier masters level education in these topics.

Year 1

The course begins in Year 1 with a set of introductory topics around these subjects. Foundations of Mathematics introduces you to some basic concepts, such as sets of numbers, maps between these sets and notation to help simplify their expression and relation. It will form the basis for the rest of the degree programme. Algebra I, Analysis I, Geometry and Linear Algebra I will provide a deep overview of the concepts of symmetry, groups, set theory, sequences & series, limits, linear transformations/matrices and vector spaces; basic tools necessary for further study.

Differential Equations I, Classicial Mechanics I and Probability I are three application areas that make use of the theoretical content studied in the aforementioned modules.

Year 2

In Year 2 the real analytical concepts introduced in Analysis I and Analysis II are extended to multi-dimensional spaces in Vector Analysis I and Vector Analysis II. In Metric Spaces more abstract settings which involve a notion of distance will be explored. Analysis III will put Riemann integration on a firmer footing.

On the algebraic side Algebra II and Linear Algebra II will introduce matrix decomposition, as well as further explore groups. Rings and modules will also briefly be touched upon. Geometry II will introduce projective geometry and more abstract geometrical entities.

There are plenty of applied options in Year 2. Differential Equations II, Classicial Mechanics II and Probability II continue where the respective Year 1 courses left off. Combinatorics and Number Theory are two more theoretical applications involving quantity as a concept. Partial Differential Equations I and Statistics I offer two more applied courses which lead onto deeper areas of mathematics.
