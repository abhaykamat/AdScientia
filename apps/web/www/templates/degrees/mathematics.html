Mathematics

Four Year Undergraduate Degree Programme - MSc/MMath

Mathematics, as a subject, concerns itself with the abstract concepts of Quantity, Structure, Shape and Change [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics]. The AdScientia Mathematics degree course provides resources equivalent to a top-tier masters level education in these topics.

Year 1

The course begins in Year 1 with a set of introductory topics around these subjects. Foundations of Mathematics introduces you to some basic concepts, such as sets of numbers, maps between these sets and notation to help simplify their expression and relation. It will form the basis for the rest of the degree programme. Algebra I, Analysis I, Geometry and Linear Algebra I will provide a deep overview of the concepts of symmetry, groups, set theory, sequences & series, limits, linear transformations/matrices and vector spaces; basic tools necessary for further study.

Differential Equations I, Classicial Mechanics I and Probability I are three application areas that make use of the theoretical content studied in the aforementioned modules.

Year 2

In Year 2 the real analytical concepts introduced in Analysis I and Analysis II are extended to multi-dimensional spaces in Vector Analysis I and Vector Analysis II. In Metric Spaces more abstract settings which involve a notion of distance will be explored. Analysis III will put Riemann integration on a firmer footing.

On the algebraic side Algebra II and Linear Algebra II will introduce matrix decomposition, as well as further explore groups. Rings and modules will also briefly be touched upon. Geometry II will introduce projective geometry and more abstract geometrical entities.

There are plenty of applied options in Year 2. Differential Equations II, Classicial Mechanics II and Probability II continue where the respective Year 1 courses left off. Combinatorics and Number Theory are two more theoretical applications involving quantity as a concept. Partial Differential Equations I and Statistics I offer two more applied courses which lead onto deeper areas of mathematics.

Year 3

In Year 3 deeper analytical, algebraic, geometric and applied modules are available.

The core courses for further studies of analysis include Complex Analysis, which concerns itself with analytical concepts such as convergence and continuity, on the complex plane; Linear Functional Analysis, which explores vector spaces in an infinite-dimensional setting; Measure Theory, which introduces alternative methods of characterising the size of sets as well as methods of integrating functions that do not permit a Riemann integral; and finally Numerical Analysis which provides a framework for quantifying error growth in certain approximation situations.

On the algebraic path courses include Galois Theory, which provides a connection between fieds and groups; Groups I, which delves deeper into group concepts beyond those elucidated in Algebra II; Rings and Modules I, which introduces the algebraic structure known as a ring; Set Theory, which outlines the axiomatic framework of mathematical sets on a more fundamental level.

The core geometry courses include Differential Geometry, which explains concepts concerning curves and surfaces in $\mathbb{R}^3$; Fractal Geometry, which studies fractals - geometric entities which may possess non-integral dimension; Knot Theory, a course which discusses mathematical knots, or topological braids; Topology, an introduction to more abstract spaces, that have no notion of distance, but where continuity can still be discussed.

The set of applied courses include continuations via Classical Mechanics III, Differential Equations III, Partial Differential Equations II and Statistics II. Continuum Mechanics and Fluid Dynamics both attempt to formulate mathematical models for solids, liquids and gases under certain types of stress and strain, being highly relevant in the fields of Physics and Engineering.

Year 4

By the start of Year 4, many core algebraic, analytical and geometric concepts will be understood. The courses in this year are designed to provide a deep investigation of certain areas of each of these strands of mathematics.
