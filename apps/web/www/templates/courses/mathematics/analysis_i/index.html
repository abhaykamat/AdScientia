Analysis I

Introduction

Analysis is an extremely important part of mathematics. It is our first formal introduction to inequalities, sequences of numbers, infinite sums of numbers (known as series) and a deep concept known as completeness.

Here are some questions we can try to answer with the theory of Real Analysis:

  • What is infinity? Are there different types of infinity?
  • What is a derivative? What is an infinitesimal quantity? Do all functions have derivatives?
  • What happens if I sum up an infinite series of numbers? Do I always get an infinite result?
  • How many numbers are there in a real number line? How many numbers are there between two real numbers?

We will begin with definitions of certain sets of numbers: the natural numbers $\mathbb{N}$, the integers $\mathbb{Z}$, the rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$ and the real numbers $\mathbb{R}$. Then we will look at inequalities. Inequalities are mathematical relations that help us determine size and ordering of various quantities. They provide the mathematical framework for bounding one quantity by another - an essential tool if we are to discuss sequences and series of numbers.

After inequalities we will study sequences of numbers. A sequence is a list of elements that may possess a pattern. The sequence may be bounded or it may continually increase or decrease in size. We will characterise when this is the case using our inequalities toolkit.

The real number line has a very interesting geometry. We will investigate it and try to determine how "infinite" the real line is. We will discuss what happens when we "cut" the real line. We will define the Axiom of Completeness and subsequently utilise it to gain better insight into the structure of the real line.

Finally, we will look at series - infinite sums of numbers. Our main task will be to determine if any series have a finite sum or whether the values sum to infinity. We can use our knowledge about sequences and inequalities to help us.

What's next?

In Analysis II, the main focus will be on functions from $\mathbb{R}$ to itself, i.e. functions $f$ of the form $f:\mathbb{R}\rightarrow\mathbb{R}$. We will study the concepts of continuity and convergence of functions as well as define the derivative from first principles.
